Varanasi: The cultural capital of India

The city that epitomises both ends of the spectrum: life and death.

About

Located in the middle-Ganges valley in the southeastern part of the state of Uttar Pradesh, Varanasi also known as Benares, Banaras or Kashi lies on the left bank of the river. Varanasi gets its name from the two streams of holy Ganges in the city – the ‘Varuna’ and the ‘assi’. According to Hindu mythology, Varanasi was founded by Shiva.In the Mahabharata the city is referred to as Kāśī (काशी: Kashi) from the Sanskrit verbal root kaś- 'to shine', making Varanasi known as 'City of Light', the 'luminous city as an eminent seat of learning'. The name was also used by pilgrims dating from Buddha's days.

It is one of the oldest living cities in the world. Mark Twain, the English author and literature, who was enthralled by the legend and sanctity of Benaras, once wrote : “Benaras is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend and looks twice as old as all of them put together”.

Varanasi has been a great center of learning for ages. The city has long been an educational and musical centre: many prominent Indian philosophers, poets, writers, and musicians live or have lived in the city, and it was the place where the Benares gharana form of Hindustani classical music was developed. Varanasi is associated with promotion of spiritualism, mysticism, Sanskrit, yoga and Hindi language and honored authors such as the ever-famous novelist Prem Chand and Tulsi Das, the famous saint-poet who wrote Ram Charit Manas. The city is a centre of arts and crafts and of music and dance. Varanasi is famous for its production of silks and brocades with gold and silver thread work. A renowned carpet-weaving centre is at Bhadoi. Wooden toys, bangles made of glass, ivory work, and brassware are also produced in Varanasi. The city is known worldwide for its many ghats, steps leading down the steep river bank to the water, where pilgrims perform rituals.


Top sights

Ganges River

Ganges in Varanasi is believed to have the power to wash away the sins of mortals

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Meaning “The Lord Of The World “, this Shiva temple is one of 12 Jyotirlingas

Sarnath

At this very place, Goutam Buddha first gave his preaching of dharma.

Dashashwamedh Ghat

Main and probably the oldest ghat of Varanasi located on the Ganges, close to the Kashi Vishwanath Temple.

Assi Ghat

Assi ghat leaves a distinctive mark on the heart of visitors and is the lifeline of locals.

Manikarnika Ghat

Crematory ghat portrays the eternal truth of life -‘Death’. According to a myth, it is said that an earring of Sati fell here.

Harishchandra Ghat

Harishchandra ghat, also called Shmashan or Mashan ghat is one of the oldest ghats of Varanasi.

Banaras Hindu University

Madan Mohan Malaviya strove to promote modern education among Indians and co-founded the Banaras Hindu University (BHU) at Varanasi in 1916, which was created under the 1915 BHU Act. Campus also has a Birla temple.

Tibetan Temple

Famous for Lord Buddha preaching his disciples here, and then dharma being carried through generations of monks at this very location.

Nepali Temple

Also Known as Kathwala temple which is Lord Shiva Temple with distinct Nepali architecture and unique Shiva Linga.

Archaeological Museum

Has very interesting items excavated including the original pillar from which we derived the national emblem.

Best time to visit

  • The best time period for visiting Varanasi is from October to March, when the weather is not so hot and it is dry. November to February is the peak season, when crowds and higher prices are expected.
  • July to September (the rainy season) is the best time for low prices in general, when you should find the biggest discounts and more promotions on accommodation, flights, and tours.
  • Consider traveling during Holi (usually in March) or Diwali (in late October or November), which are the two grandest festivals in Varanasi and also in India.
  • Festival time -
    • Mahashivaratri, the great night of the god Shiva, is celebrated by a procession from the Mahamrityunjaya Temple to the Kashi Vishvanatha (Vishwanath) Temple.
    • The Ganga festival in November or December is dedicated to the goddess of the Ganges River, considered sacred by all Hindus. Thousands of lamps are placed on the ghats and set afloat on the river.
    • The festival of Bharat Milap in October or November commemorates the reunion of Lord Rama with his younger brother Bharat after 14 years of exile.
    • A five-day festival of dhrupad (classical Indian vocal style) in March attracts renowned artists from all over India to the city’s Tulsi Ghat along the river.


Activities to do

Evening Aarti of Mother Ganga

The Ganga Aarti Ceremony held every evening at the Dasashwamedh Ghat at Varanasi is one of the most spectacular religious or devotional rituals carried out in the world.

Morning Boat Ride in Ganges River

The best time to enjoy boat ride of the Ganges River is from 5:30 AM to 8 AM. It usually starts from Dasawamedh Ghat, goes to the Harishchandra Ghat and then back.

Take a Holy Bath in Ganges

River Ganges is considered to the holiest Hindu River. Taking a dip in the holy waters here will offer salvation of sins and purification of the soul to purify all sins accumulated in current or previous births.

Shopping in Local Market

The city is widely famous for its silk saris and ornate jewels, the intricate pattern of floral designs and elaborate zari or thread work saris.

Visiting the Temples

The most popular temples to see here are Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Durga Temple, Annapurna Temple, Tulsi Manas Temple, Sankatmochan Temple etc.

Take a Stroll on the Ghats

The city has almost a hundred Ghats, the steps of each leading the banks of River Ganges.
